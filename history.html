History of The Redeemed Christian Church of God

Pa Josiah Akindayomi (Founder)

The Redeemed Christian Church of God (RCCG) was founded in 1952 by Rev. Josiah Olufemi Akindayomi, a man whose heart was set ablaze with an unquenchable desire to encounter God. Born in Ondo State, Nigeria, in 1909, his pursuit led him to the Church Missionary Society, where he was baptised in 1927. Still spiritually unfulfilled, he later joined the Cherubim and Seraphim Church. He initially ignored God's call to ministry for several years, but after repeated business failures brought him to a point of humble repentance, he fully yielded to God's will.

By 1947, Rev. Akindayomi had grown concerned with some doctrines of his previous church and established an independent prayer fellowship, "Egbe Ogo Oluwa" (The Glory of God Group), before formally separating in 1952. This house fellowship in Lagos soon became a hub for spiritual conversions and miracles. During this time, though he could not read or write, he received a divine vision in which the words "The Redeemed Christian Church of God" were written on a blackboard. By supernatural grace, he transcribed the letters, and the name was confirmed.

From its inception, the RCCG was built on a strong emphasis on prayer, evangelism, and holiness. In a spiritual encounter, God revealed to Rev. Akindayomi that the church would spread to the ends of the earth and that Jesus Christ would meet the church at His return. The RCCG was founded on a covenant: as long as its members remain obedient to God's Word, the Lord has promised to miraculously meet the needs of the Church. Rev. Akindayomi remained faithful to this vision until he went to be with the Lord in 1980.

In the early 1970s, the Lord had told Rev. Akindayomi about his successor: a young, educated man who was not yet a member. Thus, when a university lecturer in Mathematics named Enoch Adejare Adeboye joined in 1973, Rev. Akindayomi spiritually discerned this was the man. Dr. Adeboye quickly became involved, serving as an interpreter for Rev. Akindayomi by translating his sermons from Yoruba to English. He was ordained as a pastor in 1975.

Before his passing in 1980, Rev. Akindayomi revealed his divine successor: Enoch Adejare Adeboye. This decision was initially met with some skepticism from older members of the church who questioned why a relatively new and highly educated member was chosen over long-standing elders. However, Akindayomi's unwavering conviction, based on divine instruction, eventually prevailed. Adeboye was ordained as the General Overseer in 1981, marking the beginning of a new era for the RCCG.

Under Pastor E.A. Adeboye’s leadership, the RCCG began to experience the phenomenal growth promised by the Lord. A man devoted to fervent prayer and an unwavering emphasis on holy living, Pastor Adeboye's vision for expansion, fueled by aggressive evangelism and powerful "Holy Ghost Services," led to an explosion in membership. His humble disposition is widely acknowledged, and in December 2008, *Newsweek* magazine named him one of the 50 most influential people on the planet.

The establishment of the Redemption Camp, a vast prayer ground along the Lagos-Ibadan Expressway, became a central hub for the church's activities, accommodating millions for major conventions. From there, the RCCG's presence spread across Africa, Europe, North America, Asia, and Australia, fulfilling Akindayomi's prophecy. Today, the RCCG operates in over 190 countries, with millions of members and tens of thousands of parishes. It has planted churches, established educational institutions, and engaged in extensive social welfare initiatives. The church continues to uphold its foundational tenets of holiness, prayer, and evangelism, maintaining its distinct identity while adapting to diverse cultural contexts around the globe. Its history is a testament to faith, obedience, and the transformative power of divine vision.

Pastor E.A. Adeboye (General Overseer)
